Intro

Welcome, traveler! Thank you for stopping by. My name is Brandon Dela Cruz. I am an inherentely curious and optimistic person fascinated by the world we live in. I graduated from the University of Hawaii at Manoa (UHM) with a Bachelors of Science in Global Environmental Science (GES) with a certificate in Geospatial Information Systems (GIS).

During my academic journey, I collaborated with GIS professionals on diverse projects. I worked with the City and County of Honolulu Storm Water Quality Division, utilizing ESRI products for visualizing nutrient concentrations in Oahu's Eastern watershed and creating a StoryMap for public outreach. Additionally, I contributed to a NASA-funded Environmental Justice project at UHM, involving data management and mining. At AECOM, I provided technical support to Engineers and Planners using ArcGIS products, generating field maps from converted CAD files and State Office of Planning data. Lastly, for my GES thesis, I employed Python and QGIS to analyze E. coli spatiotemporal variability in an urban wetland. These experiences have equipped me with a strong foundation in GIS and Data Analysis, ready to contribute to meaningful projects in the field.
